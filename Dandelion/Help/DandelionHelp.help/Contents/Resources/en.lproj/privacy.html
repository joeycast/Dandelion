Privacy

Privacy isn't just a feature of Dandelion—it's the foundation. Your thoughts are yours alone.

Your Words Are Never Saved

This is the core promise of Dandelion: your words exist only in the moment. When you release, your text is transformed into the visual animation and then discarded. It's not:

  • Saved to your device
  • Uploaded to any server
  • Stored in any database
  • Backed up anywhere
  • Logged or recorded in any form

Once you see those seeds drift away, your words are truly gone.

This is intentional The ephemeral nature of Dandelion is by design. It lets you write without self-censorship, knowing that your words won't come back to haunt you or be discovered by anyone.

What We Store

To provide History, Insights, and prompt customization features, Dandelion stores minimal metadata on your device. If iCloud Sync is enabled in Dandelion, this data can sync across your devices:

  • Timestamp — When the release happened
  • Word count — How many words you wrote
  • Prompt preferences — Your custom prompts and which default prompts are enabled

This sync data stays on your device and in your personal iCloud account.

Other preferences (like appearance, haptics, and reminder settings) are stored locally per device. Bloom purchase state is handled by Apple App Store entitlements.

Anonymous Global Stats

If Contribute to Global Stats is enabled (enabled by default when iCloud is available, opt-out in Settings), Dandelion writes anonymous aggregate usage totals to CloudKit's public database so the app can show community totals.

  • How many releases happened
  • How many words were released
  • The hour window when those totals occurred

These records do not include your writing text, prompt content, account identifiers, or device identifiers.

No Third-Party Tracking

Dandelion has no third-party analytics or ad tracking. We don't:

  • Collect analytics about your usage
  • Track your behavior
  • Use ad networks or profiling SDKs

Cloud storage and sync are handled by Apple CloudKit.

iCloud and CloudKit

If you're signed in to iCloud, Dandelion uses CloudKit for private sync data and for anonymous global aggregates used in the in-app global counters.

  • Private sync data is stored in your personal iCloud account
  • Anonymous aggregate totals are stored in CloudKit public database records
  • Private sync data is not accessible to the Dandelion team
  • Both are subject to Apple's privacy and security protections

If iCloud is unavailable, global stats contribution is automatically disabled until iCloud is available again.

Microphone Access

Dandelion can optionally use your microphone to detect when you blow to release. This audio:

  • Is processed entirely on your device
  • Is never recorded or stored
  • Is never transmitted anywhere
  • Is only used to detect the blow gesture

You can deny microphone access and use the manual release button instead. The experience is just as meaningful.

Notifications

Dandelion can optionally send local daily reminders to help you return and release. These notifications:

  • Are scheduled on-device
  • Use curated app copy, not your writing text
  • Can be disabled any time in Settings

Purchases

Dandelion Bloom purchases are handled entirely by Apple through the App Store. We don't collect or store any payment information.

Questions?

If you have questions about privacy in Dandelion, please reach out through the app's feedback option or visit our website.

Our philosophy We built Dandelion because we believe some thoughts are meant to be released, not recorded. Your mind deserves a safe space to process without permanence.
